Research

My PhD is anchored in a simple but consequential observation: biological aging models are trained on the wrong people. Every major aging clock — proteomic, epigenomic, immunological — was built on European cohorts. Applied to South Asians, they confuse population-specific biology with aging signal. This is a distribution shift problem with real clinical consequences for 1.4 billion people.

The BHARAT Study

The BHARAT Study (Biomarkers of Healthy Aging, Resilience, Adversity, and Transitions) is a cross-sectional, multi-modal, multi-omics cohort of participants across five age strata (18–100+ years) in India. It integrates plasma proteomics via mass spectrometry-based data-independent acquisition, deep immunophenotyping, blood biochemistry, genomics, epigenomics, and metabolomics with clinical phenotyping. I lead the multi-omics and data science arm.

PhD Thesis

Building population-scale multi-omics foundations for biological age modeling in South Asian populations.

The methodological contribution is the development of novel mathematical and computational frameworks for cross-population multi-omics integration — addressing the distribution shift between European training data and South Asian target populations. The mathematical space includes latent factor models, multi-view representation learning, domain adaptation, and optimal transport approaches. The biological contribution is the first validated multi-omics aging reference for Indians. The long-term ambition is population-inclusive foundation models for aging and health.

Wet Lab Work

Alongside the computational work, I run the immunophenotyping and proteomics streams of BHARAT. Deep immune profiling via standardised flow cytometry panels characterises immune cell composition, activation states, and senescence markers across age strata. Mass spectrometry-based plasma proteomics on the timsTOF HT resolves protein-level aging trajectories in Indian biology.

Prior Research

My MTech at IISc (2022–2024) focused on cellular senescence and cell state transitions, combining cell culture models, timelapse imaging, single-cell RNA-seq analysis, and dynamical systems modeling. My undergraduate dissertation at Basaveshwar Engineering College investigated glioma pathobiology through serum metabolomics. Most of my early career was spent in mass spectrometry-based proteomics at the Proteomics Lab, IIT Bombay, where I contributed to two peer-reviewed publications on glioma heterogeneity.
